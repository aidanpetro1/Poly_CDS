# PolyCDS v1.1 — A Categorical Summary

*Aidan Petrovich · 2026-04-28*

This document summarizes the category theory underlying PolyCDS v1.1, the
project-specific reframings that earn their keep, and the inventory of
load-bearing ideas worth preserving as we move into v1.2 and beyond. It is
written for a reader comfortable with polynomial functors, comonoids, and
bicomodules in **Poly**; it does not re-derive standard material.

---

## 1. Aesthetic and goals

The driving aesthetic of PolyCDS is **CDS-as-data**: a clinical
decision-support guideline should be a categorical artifact whose
properties (coherence, joinability, refinement) hold by construction, not
an imperative procedure that we audit case-by-case.

A v1.1 guideline lives as a single algebraic object — a bicomodule
$A : S \rightleftharpoons P$ in **Poly** — whose left coaction routes
patient-result events into phenotype updates, and whose right coaction
recommends orders. Two guidelines compose by tensor product:

$$
A_{\text{joint}} \;:=\; A_{D_1} \otimes A_{D_2}
$$

is the joint CDS, formally; there is no orchestration code mediating
between them. The whole project is a wager that this aesthetic carries its
own weight — that the structure forces the right phenomena rather than
ornamenting them.

---

## 2. The observation polynomial

Each atomic observation $o$ with result type $R(o)$ is encoded as the
representable polynomial $y^{R(o)}$. Per-disease and joint observation
polynomials follow compositional structure:

$$
p_{D_k} \;=\; \sum_{o \in O_{D_k}} y^{R(o)} \qquad
p_{\text{obs}} \;=\; p_{D_1} \otimes p_{D_2}
$$

The coproduct within a disease says "clinician picks one of these tests at
each step"; the Dirichlet/Cartesian product across diseases says "each
disease's workup advances independently." The product is *the right
encoding* because both sides genuinely have separate observational
trajectories that interleave but don't share state.

Identifying observations with representables means a clinician's "did I
order the test, and what did I get back" is reducible to the categorical
data of "pick a position of $p_{\text{obs}}$, then read off a direction" —
no extra book-keeping needed.

---

## 3. Comonoids in **Poly**: histories and protocols

We need two distinct comonoids on the observation polynomial:

- $S$ — the **history comonoid**. Carrier records "where we are" in an
  observation history (a behavior tree); counit forgets back to a single
  phenotype; comultiplication splits a history into prefix-then-rest.
- $P$ — the **protocol comonoid**. Carrier records "where we are in a
  planned pathway"; counit forgets back to the order; comultiplication
  splits a pathway into step-then-remainder.

The universal history comonoid is the **cofree comonoid**
$\text{cofree}(p_{\text{obs}}, n)$ — the comonoid freely generated by
$p_{\text{obs}}$ at depth $n$. Its positions are behavior trees of
$p_{\text{obs}}$ of bounded depth; directions at a tree are paths through
the tree.

### Sub-cofree as a deliberate compromise

For tractability, v1.1 uses a **sub-cofree** comonoid $S_D$ per disease —
the sub-comonoid of $\text{cofree}(p_{D_k}, 2)$ whose positions are
exactly the protocol-relevant trees (≈4 per disease). We trade
cofree's universal property — which would give us patient-as-Coalgebra
for free — in exchange for tractability. The duplicator and counit
restrict; the comonoid laws still validate.

This is a *deliberate* compromise: the sub-cofree carrier is exactly
the smallest one whose direction structure matches the clinical phenotype
distinctions we care about. Cofree's universality is overkill for v1.1,
and it's the wrong level of abstraction to optimize against — what
matters is that **the path structure of $S_D$ is forced by clinical reality**,
not generic.

### Discrete $P$ as a v1.1 simplification

For v1.1, $P_D$ is the **discrete comonoid** on the per-disease order
vocabulary. Every order is its own object; the only morphisms are
identities. This is a known-incomplete simplification: the full payoff
of "bicomodule axiom = guideline coherence" requires $P$ to be a
free protocol-category with length-$\geq 2$ composites (see §6, §10).

---

## 4. Bicomodules and the CDS guideline

Given comonoids $S$ and $P$ in **Poly**, a **bicomodule**
$A : S \rightleftharpoons P$ is a polynomial $A$ together with:

- a **left coaction** $\lambda_L : A \to S \,\triangleright\, A$,
- a **right coaction** $\lambda_R : A \to A \,\triangleright\, P$,

(where $\triangleright$ is polynomial substitution) satisfying
coassociativity, counitality, and the **bicomodule compatibility law**:

$$
(\text{id}_S \triangleright \lambda_R) \circ \lambda_L \;=\; (\lambda_L \triangleright \text{id}_P) \circ \lambda_R
$$

Both sides are maps $A \to S \triangleright A \triangleright P$. The law
says: applying $\lambda_L$ then $\lambda_R$ on the inner $A$ agrees with
applying $\lambda_R$ first then $\lambda_L$ on the outer $A$.

### Reading the coactions clinically

$$
\lambda_R(x) \;=\; (x,\ \bar m_R^x), \qquad
\bar m_R^x : A.\text{directions}(x) \to P.\text{positions}
$$

$\bar m_R^x$ recommends a planned order at each A-direction. The slot
$\bar m_R^x[\,\texttt{:stay}\,]$ is the **current emission** — the order to
issue *while staying* in $x$. Other slots describe the post-routing
recommendation along that direction; the simulator only ever consults
$\bar m_R^x[\,\texttt{:stay}\,]$ to choose what order to issue.

$$
\lambda_L(x) \;=\; (i_S^x,\ \bar m_L^x), \qquad
\bar m_L^x : S.\text{paths-at-}i_S^x \to A.\text{positions}
$$

$i_S^x$ is the cofree behavior tree at $x$ (which the sub-cofree forces
to be exactly the protocol-relevant subtree); $\bar m_L^x$ routes paths
through the tree to next phenotypes. Length-1 paths route single test
results; length-2 paths route panels of two consecutive tests; the empty
path routes to $x$ itself.

### Phenotypes as A-positions (key reframe)

The **positions of $A$** are *computable phenotypes* — not generic
"interpretations." Specifically, sub-cofree-S forces the phenotype set to
match clinical phenotype-discrimination: distinct A-positions exist for

- $a_{D_k,\,\text{initial}}$, $a_{D_k,\,\text{pending}}$,
- $a_{D_k}$ (confirmed),
- $a_{D_k,\,\text{absent\_via\_}o_{1a}}$, $a_{D_k,\,\text{absent\_via\_}o_{1b}}$
  (absent-by-screen vs absent-by-confirmation).

The phenotype set is exactly the smallest one that distinguishes all
clinically-meaningful workup states. Cofree-S's path structure forces
this — we cannot collapse phenotypes without breaking the comonoid laws.
This is a striking feature: **the formalism resists the temptation to
under-discriminate**.

## 5. Bicomodule axiom = guideline coherence

The bicomodule compatibility law says result-driven routing
($\lambda_L$) and planned-order pathway ($\lambda_R$) agree on the joint
$S \triangleright A \triangleright P$.

Concretely: at phenotype $x$, suppose
$\bar m_R^x[\,\texttt{:stay}\,] = p$ (recommended order) and a result
triggers an S-path $b$ landing at next phenotype
$\bar m_L^x(b) = x'$. The compatibility law forces $x'$'s right coaction
to be consistent with what $p$'s next P-step recommends. **Internal
coherence of the guideline is the bicomodule compatibility law.**

In v1.1 with discrete $P$, the law holds trivially — there are no
composite morphisms in $P$ for the law to constrain. The insight is
*deferred, not retracted*. With $P$ promoted to a free protocol-category
in v1.2, the law has real work to do, and `validate_bicomodule_detailed`
becomes a non-trivial coherence proof on each candidate guideline.

The clinical reading: the law catches the case "this guideline says do A
then B if A-positive, but the post-A-positive interpretation
recommends C instead." That kind of inconsistency is exactly what
clinicians manually audit; the categorical machinery makes the
audit a single pass over a structure.

## 6. The tensor product of bicomodules — joint CDS

Given $A : S \rightleftharpoons P$ and $A' : S' \rightleftharpoons P'$,
their **tensor product** is the bicomodule

$$
A \otimes A' \;:\; (S \otimes S') \rightleftharpoons (P \otimes P')
$$

with carrier $A.\text{positions} \times A'.\text{positions}$, joint
directions = pairs of per-side directions, and coactions glued via the
comonoid tensor.

For PolyCDS:

$$
A_{\text{joint}} \;:=\; A_{D_1} \otimes A_{D_2} \;:\; (S_{D_1} \otimes S_{D_2}) \rightleftharpoons (P_{D_1} \otimes P_{D_2})
$$

This *is* the categorical ground truth of the joint CDS. There is no
orchestration code; the joint guideline is the formal $\otimes$ of
per-disease guidelines.

A historical note worth preserving: the eager construction of the joint
duplicator's substitution polynomial was combinatorially intractable
(positions $\propto |q|^{|p|}$ in the substitution $p \triangleright q$).
Lazifying $\triangleright$ at the level of `Lens.cod`-style structural
witnesses removes the obstruction; the formal $\otimes$ is constructible
in seconds on v1.1's carriers (≈25 joint phenotypes). **Without lazy
substitution, joint comonoids of any realistic size are
unconstructable**, and the formal-$\otimes$ aesthetic collapses back to
hand-built orchestration. This is a load-bearing design dependency
between the two repos.

## 7. Direction labels and the panel-vs-sequential dichotomy

A cofree-S length-1 path encodes a single observation event; a length-2
path encodes a panel of two events. PolyCDS labels these directions
clinically:

- $\texttt{:stay}$ — empty path; patient is not acted upon
- $\texttt{:seq\_neg}, \texttt{:seq\_pos}$ — single-event paths
- $\texttt{:panel\_pos\_neg}, \texttt{:panel\_pos\_pos}$ — two-event paths,
  first positive

The $\texttt{:panel\_neg\_*}$ cases are *absent by structure* in the
carrier: in real clinical practice a screen-negative result terminates
workup before a confirmation is ordered. This is encoded in the cofree
subtree we pick at each phenotype; the formal structure refuses to
recommend a confirmation after a negative screen.

The simulator's `mode` parameter ($\texttt{:sequential} \mid \texttt{:panel}$)
is a **policy for picking joint A-directions through $A_{\text{joint}}$**.
Both modes traverse the same bicomodule; mode controls path length and
observation ordering, not the destination phenotype. The fact that
both modes converge to the same final phenotype is the formal content of
"any test ordering reaches the same diagnostic conclusion."

The `:sequential` and `:panel` axes are aligned at the per-disease level
(which A-direction within a side) and the joint level (which side
advances per step). Aligning them gives two clean demos; the two
non-aligned combinations are reachable with finer-grained policies but
are clinically idiosyncratic and v1.1 does not surface them.

## 8. Patient as responder

A patient is, formally, a **section** of $p_{\text{obs}}$ — a function
from each obs-position to a result-direction:

$$
\sigma_{\text{patient}} : p_{\text{obs}}.\text{positions} \to \text{Set}^{\to}, \qquad
\sigma(o) \in R(o)
$$

In v1.1 we represent this as a struct with a `respond` function rather
than as a Coalgebra for $p_{\text{obs}}$ (a Coalgebra signature
$X \to p_{\text{obs}}(X)$ would force a state space, which a stateless
deterministic patient lacks).

The cofree-S structure is the universal home for sectional-patient: a
Coalgebra over $\text{cofree}(p_{\text{obs}}, n)$ is a behavior tree.
Stateless deterministic patients are constant-strategy behavior trees;
v2's FHIR-substrate patients become stateful coalgebras over the same
cofree.

The asymmetry between the patient (responder) and the simulator
(chooser) mirrors the clinical asymmetry: the clinician picks tests, the
patient's biology answers them. Encoding this asymmetry structurally —
rather than as control flow in the simulator — is part of the aesthetic
payoff.

## 9. Settled choices

These are the v1.1 design commitments worth preserving across future
revisions. Each is load-bearing in the sense that abandoning it would
either break the categorical aesthetic or the clinical interpretation.

1. **Phenotypes as A-positions**, not generic interpretations. Cofree-S's
   path structure forces clinical-reality phenotype discrimination.
2. **Sub-cofree S** for tractability + ragged-$T_{\text{root}}$ to drop
   the off-protocol phenotype.
3. **$\bar m_R^x[\,\texttt{:stay}\,]$ as the current emission slot.**
   Distinguishes "what to issue while staying" from "what's recommended
   after routing along a direction."
4. **Mode as simulator policy, not structural.** Both `:sequential` and
   `:panel` are valid trajectories through the same $A_{\text{joint}}$.
5. **Joint via formal $\otimes$**, not orchestration; not a hand-built
   sub-comonoid $S_{\text{joint}}$. The reachability-restriction
   workaround was considered and ruled out once lazy substitution made
   it unnecessary.
6. **Patient as struct + `respond`**, not Coalgebra in v1.1 (the type
   signature mismatch is real; Coalgebra fits naturally only at v2 with
   stateful FHIR-substrate patients).
7. **Recommendations from $\lambda_R.\text{on\_positions}$**, not
   hand-typed lookup tables. The categorical structure carries the
   recommendation; we do not duplicate it.

## 10. Parked items and v1.2+ paths

### Deferred to v1.2

- **Free-P with length-$\geq 2$ composites.** Restores the
  "bicomodule axiom = guideline coherence" payoff. Requires a matching
  *"extend by length(P-morph) S-steps"* rule for $\sharp_R$, which is
  structurally cleaner than the v1.1 thin-P attempt that broke
  coassociativity.
- **Off-protocol alarm.** Was a free byproduct of the depth-2 cofree
  $T_{\text{neg}}$ subtree; lost when we moved to ragged sub-cofree.
  v1.2 may re-introduce as redundant-test detection.
- **Chief complaint / adaptive initial differential.** The sub-cofree
  $S$ can express this (via a CC-keyed initial tree set) but doubles
  phenotype work; deferred.
- **`:refer_*` orders.** No phenotype currently recommends a referral;
  vocabulary entry is structurally meaningless in v1.1.

### Future (v2 and beyond)

- **FHIR copresheaf.** Patient state lives in a copresheaf
  $F : \mathcal{C} \to \text{Set}$ on the FHIR schema category; v2's
  patient is a Coalgebra over this, not a plain struct.
- **Cross-linked bicomodules $M \odot N$** for sub-workups (e.g., a
  shared lab panel reused across multiple disease workups).
- **Wiring diagrams** as a visual presentation of bicomodule
  composition, useful for clinician-facing protocol authoring.
- **Probability via Markov categories.** Stochastic patients and
  prior-laden differential diagnosis without stuffing $[0,1]$ into
  directions (which would break the polynomial-functor structure).

## 11. Inventory of load-bearing ideas

These are the moves that make the categorical aesthetic actually
*productive* on this domain — the places where the formalism doesn't
just permit a clinical phenomenon but forces it.

1. **Cofree-S forces clinical-reality phenotypes.** The sub-cofree
   carrier's path structure exactly mirrors the workups a clinician
   distinguishes; $\bar m_L$ becomes a forced map, not a hand-table.
2. **`:stay` as the recommendation slot.** Cleanly distinguishes the
   pre-transition emission from post-transition recommendations. Without
   this convention, $\bar m_R$ would conflate two different roles.
3. **One $A_D$ exposes both seq and panel.** A single per-disease
   bicomodule, with the right cofree subtree at each phenotype, supports
   both modes via different direction choices. We do not build separate
   "sequential" and "panel" guidelines.
4. **Lazy substitution unlocks the formal $\otimes$.** Without it, joint
   comonoids of any realistic size are unconstructable, and the
   formal-$\otimes$ aesthetic collapses to hand-built orchestration.
5. **Bicomodule compatibility = guideline coherence.** The categorical
   law lines up exactly with the clinical property "result-driven
   updates agree with planned pathways." The mathematics earns its
   keep at the level of *what the law says*, not just at the level of
   *what types it constrains*.
6. **Joint = formal $\otimes$, not orchestration.** The categorical
   artifact IS the joint CDS. We do not write code that drives two
   per-disease bicomodules in tandem.
7. **Mode = simulator policy, not structural distinction.** Different
   traversals of the same bicomodule. Generalizes cleanly to $n$ modes
   if other workup styles emerge.
8. **Phenotype-naming carries discrimination structure.** Names like
   $a_{D,\,\text{absent\_via\_}o_{1a}}$ vs $a_{D,\,\text{absent\_via\_}o_{1b}}$
   capture *which test ruled the disease out*, which is exactly the
   discriminating structure cofree-S forces.
9. **Discrete $P$ as honest deferral.** Acknowledging that v1.1's
   bicomodule axiom holds trivially is not a flaw — it's a marker of
   where the substantive work lives in v1.2. The structure is in place;
   only the protocol-category needs to be filled in.

---

## 12. Summary

PolyCDS v1.1 demonstrates that a clinically meaningful CDS guideline can
live as a single formal object — a bicomodule $A : S \rightleftharpoons P$
in **Poly** — whose internal structure (cofree-derived $S$, hand-crafted
phenotype $A$, deliberate simplification of $P$) forces clinical reality
where the formalism is most productive, and defers honestly where it
isn't yet. The joint CDS is the formal tensor product of per-disease
guidelines, made tractable by lazy substitution. The simulator is a thin
policy layer over a single categorical artifact.

The substantive coherence payoff — bicomodule compatibility law as
guideline-coherence proof — is on hold until $P$ is upgraded to a free
protocol-category in v1.2, but the architecture for it is already
present. v1.1 is a structurally complete prototype with a single
named TODO.
